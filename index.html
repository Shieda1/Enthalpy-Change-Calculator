What is the enthalpy change?
The entalpy change is the difference between total reactant and total product molar enthalpies and it is calculatd ofr the substances in their standard states.
How to calculate enthalpy change?
To calculate enthalpy change you should use the following formula:
ΔHr^Φ = ΣB vB * ΔHf^Φ * (B)
where:
ΔHr^Φ = standard enthaply of reaction
B = entity B
vB = stoichimoetric number
ΔHf^Φ = standard enthalpy of formation
Why use enthalpy change?
Calculating the enthalpy change helps in determing whether a reaction was endothermic (positive change in enthalpy, absorbed heat) or exothermic (negative change in enthalpy, released heat). In other words it is used to calculate the heat of reaction of a chemical process.
